NP Nurse Companion Project

The NP Nurse Companion project is an ongoing initiative in collaboration with the Michigan School of Nursing, aiming to support newly qualified Nurse Practitioners (NPs) during their transition into practice. High turnover rates among NPs within their first year are often attributed to a lack of support and mentorship. To address this issue, our team is developing a web-based, mobile-first application that facilitates mentorship and provides relevant resources.

Key Features

  • Mentorship Matching: An algorithm will connect new NPs with experienced mentors based on factors such as years of experience, specialty, geographic location, and preferred communication methods.
  • Progress Tracking: A timeline feature will guide users through their first two years, offering encouragement and insights at critical milestones.
  • Communication Tools: Integrated chat functions will enable interactions between mentors and mentees, as well as among peer NPs, fostering a supportive community.
  • Resource Library: Curated content will provide guidance on what to expect in the new role, supplemented by a Q&A interface to address common concerns.

Development Process

Our development process emphasizes user experience, utilizing tools like Figma for prototyping and flutter for cross-platform compatibility. We are also committed to data privacy and compliance, ensuring that all collected information adheres to relevant regulations, including HIPAA.

Next Steps

Upon completion, we plan to pilot the NP Companion app with a cohort of new Nurse Practitioners to evaluate its effectiveness and gather user feedback for further refinement.
