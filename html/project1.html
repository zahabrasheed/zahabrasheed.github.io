Mahad al Zahra Conduct Tracker

The Problem: A Broken System for Behavior Tracking

Teachers at Mahad Al Zahra struggled with an outdated system—an Excel sheet—to track student behavior. The result? Inconsistent reporting, frustrated parents, and teachers unable to provide clear, objective insights. Parents often doubted reports, unsure if teachers were exaggerating or downplaying their child's behavior. This lack of transparency eroded trust between educators and families.

We needed a better way: A seamless, reliable platform that required minimal technical skills and kept all stakeholders informed.

Our Approach: Building a Simple Yet Powerful Tool

To solve this, we designed the Hogwarts Style Point Tracker, a web application that allows teachers to log both positive and negative behaviors in real time, automatically generating clear reports for parents.

  • Key Challenge: Teachers had varying levels of technological expertise, so the tool had to be extremely simple to use.
  • Research: We conducted interviews with teachers to understand their frustrations and needs.
  • Core Design Decisions:
    • A one-page form for quick behavior tracking.
    • Mobile-first design for easy access.
    • Automated reporting to ensure accuracy and transparency.

Iterating for Success

Initially, we assumed the system would primarily be for only logging. However, feedback revealed that teachers wanted the ability to engage students in healthy competition. They also wanted to send reports and look at student behavior ovet time. This led us to create a user friendly reports page, with automated emailing capability to parents.

Expected Impact: A Trusted Communication Bridge

Although full implementation is still in progress, early feedback suggests significant improvements:

  • High teacher adoption potential, based on usability testing.
  • Ease of Use Score: Positive preliminary responses from teachers.
  • Report accuracy expected to improve, reducing inconsistencies in student records.
  • Increased parent trust, with initial feedback praising clarity and transparency.

Moving forward, we plan to integrate fully automated monthly emails to refine the platform further and ensure it continues to meet user needs.

Final Thoughts

This project was all about finding the right balance between user experience and functionality. By keeping things simple, transparent, and efficient, we built a tool that can really improve communication between teachers and parents, making student behavior tracking clearer and more trustworthy. This was also my first time working in the UI/UX space, and it pushed me to think differently—not just about how I build things, but about how design and software work together to shape the way people interact with technology.
