Revitalizing Dr. Strassmann's Digital Legacy

The Problem: Dr. Strassmann's research in Biological Anthropology was overshadowed by an outdated website that lacked clear navigation, accessibility, and engagement.

Key problems included:

  • Lack of visibility for research collaborators and ongoing projects.
  • Poor readability and organization, making it difficult for students and researchers to explore her work.
  • Outdated design, failing to reflect the impact of her contributions.

    • Solution: Create a modern, accessible, and research-focused website that accurately represents her contributions and makes her work more discoverable to both academic and public audiences.

    This project was part of the Undergraduate Research Opportunities Program and culminated in a final research poster at the University of Michigan's Spring Symposium, where I presented insights on designing effective academic websites.

    Process: A Collaborative, Iterative Approach

    To ensure the final design met both academic and usability standards , we followed a structured yet flexible approach:

    • Content Strategy & Research - Identified key sections: Research Areas, People, Publications, and Contact.
    • Wireframing & Prototyping - Designed initial layouts in Figma based on user needs
    • Iterative Feedback & Refinement - Reviewed progress with Dr. Strassmann, refining the structure and design.
    • Accessibility & Visual Optimization - Adjusted color contrast, typography, and layout to meet usability standards.
    • Final Implementation - Developed the website using WordPress, ensuring a seamless user experience.

    Key Design Elements

    In the first iteration of prototyping we focused on updating the content and creating a basic updated layout. We did not focus as much on the color and aesthetics as we wanted to have a MVP website for Dr. Strassmann before a confrence she attended. Although we were unsatisfied with the colors chosen, we focused more on content than UI at this stage.

    In the first iteration of prototyping, we prioritized content strategy and developed a streamlined, user-friendly layout. Rather than focusing on visual hierarchy and UI aesthetics, our goal was to create an MVP (Minimum Viable Product) website for Dr. Strassmann ahead of an important conference. While we were unsatisfied with the color palette and overall UI consistency, we focused on information architecture and ensuring content accessibility at this stage.

    In the second iteration of prototyping, we focused on refining UI elements, including the color palette, and making the website more concise. Our goal was to give users a clear overview of Dr. Strassmann's research while allowing them to explore deeper if they chose to. Based on user testing, we streamlined the research section, as it was overly wordy. Explaining the research naturally led to dense blocks of text, which we condensed in the next iteration for better readability and engagement.

    When refining the color palette, we wanted to pay homage to Mali and the location of Dr. Strassmann's research, making the website feel more cohesive with the photographs we planned to feature from her travels. We chose a terracotta shade to reflect the reddish tones of the ground, creating a more natural and connected visual experience.

    Results & Early Impact

    While the website is still in development, initial feedback highlights:

    • Enhanced user experience - Usability tests show improved navigation and content clarity.
    • Stronger academic engagement - Increased accessibility makes research more discoverable.
    • Recognition for Dr. Strassmann's work - Early reviews emphasize the site's improved presentation of her research.

    Final Thoughts and Key Takeaways

    Collaboration is key - Regular feedback cycles with Dr. Strassmann helped refine the design.
    Iteration leads to improvement- designs weren't perfect, but continuous refinements led to a strong final product.
    Simplicity enhances usability - A clean, organized layout makes complex research more accessible.
    By prioritizing clarity, accessibility, and academic rigor, we transformed Dr. Strassmann's website into an engaging, user-friendly platform that truly reflects her research legacy.
